巴菲特的现金堆
真的能预测美股大跌吗？

Does Buffett's Cash Pile
Actually Predict Crashes?

炒股圈流传一种说法：每当伯克希尔现金持仓创新高，美股就要大跌。本文用 1998–2024 年的实际数据检验它。

A widely circulated claim says: when Berkshire Hathaway hoards cash, the S&P 500 is about to crash. We test it against actual data from 1998 to 2024.

基于 7 个历史事件 · 1998–2024 · 数据更新于 2026 年 5 月 7 historical events · 1998–2024 · Data updated May 2026
结论先行 Bottom Line

这是一个典型的叙事偏见放大的模式识别错觉。把伯克希尔现金峰值当作"大跌信号"的命中率为 14.3%，与历史上任意 12 个月窗口出现 ≥10% 回撤的无条件概率（13–15%）几乎完全一致。这个"信号"不携带任何超出基准的预测信息。

This is a textbook case of narrative-driven pattern illusion. The hit rate of Berkshire's cash peaks predicting a market drop is 14.3% — virtually identical to the unconditional baseline (13–15%) of any random 12-month window producing a ≥10% drawdown. The "signal" carries no information beyond chance.

流言是怎么说的 What the Myth Says

这种说法在中文股票论坛、英文 Twitter/X 和 YouTube 财经频道反复出现，最近一次大流行是在 2024 年第三季度——伯克希尔现金占总资产比例升至 28%，创 34 年新高。[7] 评论区涌现大量"巴菲特囤现金 = 崩盘倒计时"式的断言。

The claim recirculates regularly on Chinese stock forums, English-language X/Twitter, and YouTube finance channels — most recently in Q3 2024, when Berkshire's cash-to-total-assets ratio hit 28%, a 34-year high. [7] Comment sections filled with variations on "Buffett is hoarding cash = the crash is coming."

这种说法的吸引力来自一个简单的故事：巴菲特是公认的价值投资大师，他比一般人更早看到泡沫，所以他大量持有现金时市场就要下跌。听起来合理——但合理的故事不等于真实的相关性。我们要检验的是：用过去 26 年的数据，这个信号到底有没有预测能力？

The appeal lies in a simple story: Buffett is the recognized master of value investing, he sees bubbles earlier than most, therefore his cash hoarding signals an impending decline. Plausible — but a plausible story is not a real correlation. The question we test: across 26 years of data, does this signal carry actual predictive power?

方法 Method

信号定义

Defining the Signal

伯克希尔现金/总资产比例（或现金/股东权益比例）出现局部峰值的所有时点，1998 年至 2024 年共 7 个事件。数据来自伯克希尔 10-K/10-Q 文件、GuruFocus 资产配置序列[1]，以及 Kobeissi Letter 整理的 34 年现金占比序列[7]。

All time points at which Berkshire's cash-to-total-assets ratio (or cash-to-shareholder-equity ratio) reached a local peak between 1998 and 2024 — seven events in total. Sources: Berkshire 10-K/10-Q filings, GuruFocus asset allocation series[1], and Kobeissi Letter's 34-year compilation[7].

结果变量

Outcome Variable

每个信号触发后 6、12、24 个月内 S&P 500 的价格回报（不含股息再投资）。"大跌"严格定义为：12 个月内累计回报 ≤ −10%。

S&P 500 price returns at 6, 12, and 24 months after each signal date (excluding dividend reinvestment). A "crash" is strictly defined as: cumulative return ≤ −10% within 12 months.

基准

Baseline

任意时点（无条件）下 S&P 500 滚动回报的历史分布，使用 Robert Shiller 长期数据集[9]。这是这个分析的关键——如果信号触发后的回报分布与无条件基准没有差异，那么"信号"就是无信息的。

The historical distribution of S&P 500 rolling returns at any (unconditional) start date, using Robert Shiller's long-run dataset[9]. This is the crux of the analysis — if the post-signal distribution does not differ from the unconditional baseline, the "signal" carries no information.

三个认知陷阱 Three Cognitive Traps

数据本身已经讲清了"信号"无效。但同样有意思的问题是：为什么这个流言这么有传播力？至少有三个机制在起作用。

The data alone disposes of the signal. The more interesting question is why the myth spreads so persistently. Three mechanisms are at work.

  1. 选择性记忆

    Selection Bias

    人们记住的是 2007 年的"完美预言"——巴菲特囤现金，第二年就崩盘了。但 1998、2005（两次）、2017、2019 这五次现金高位之后市场继续上涨数十个百分点，全被遗忘。这是确认偏误（confirmation bias）的教科书案例。2005 Q1 的现金占比是史上最高之一，结果之后 24 个月 S&P 500 上涨 20%——但你几乎不会在社交媒体上看到这个事实被提起。

    What people remember is the 2007 "perfect prediction" — Buffett hoarded cash, then everything crashed. The five other instances (1998, 2005a, 2005b, 2017, 2019) where high cash was followed by tens of percent of further gains have been forgotten. Textbook confirmation bias. Q1 2005 was one of the highest cash ratios in Berkshire's history; the S&P 500 rose 20% over the next 24 months — yet you'll almost never see that mentioned in the social-media versions of this story.

  2. 内生性混淆

    Endogeneity Confusion

    巴菲特囤现金的真实原因——他自己反复说过——是找不到便宜买点[5]。这本质上是对市场估值过高的同义反复。所以现金占比与 Buffett Indicator (市值/GDP) 和 Shiller CAPE 都高度相关。即使现金峰值后真的跌了，正确的因果链是：

    Buffett's real reason for hoarding cash — as he has stated repeatedly — is that he can't find anything attractively priced[5]. This is essentially a tautology with high market valuation. So cash share is highly correlated with the Buffett Indicator (market cap / GDP) and the Shiller CAPE. Even when crashes do follow, the causal chain is:

    高估值 → 同时驱动 (a) 巴菲特找不到买点 + (b) 未来回报偏低 High valuation → simultaneously drives (a) Buffett can't find buys + (b) lower future returns

    这两个共同后果被误读成因果——这是经典的混杂变量问题。真正的预测因子是估值（Shiller CAPE、Buffett Indicator），不是现金堆。后者只是前者的嘈杂代理变量。

    These two co-effects get misread as cause and effect — a classic confounder problem. The actual predictor is valuation (Shiller CAPE, Buffett Indicator), not the cash pile. The latter is just a noisy proxy for the former.

  3. 时滞不固定

    Variable Lag

    即使在最有利于这个流言的 2005–2008 案例中：现金信号最早出现在 2005 Q1，市场顶点出现在 2007 Q4，间隔近 33 个月。如果一位投资者真的在 2005 看到信号就清仓，他要么先错过 2005–2007 的全部涨幅（机会成本巨大），要么熬不住后又重新进场——这正是为什么实际可执行的策略价值为零。"会跌但不知道什么时候"等于"无法用于决策"。

    Even in the case most favorable to the myth — 2005–2008 — the cash signal first appeared in Q1 2005; the market top arrived in Q4 2007, nearly 33 months later. An investor who sold on the 2005 signal would either miss the entire 2005–2007 rally (massive opportunity cost) or capitulate and re-enter. Either way, the practical value of the signal is zero. "Will eventually fall, timing unknown" is operationally equivalent to "unusable for decisions."

一个更诚实的版本 A More Honest Version

如果一定要从这个现象里榨出有用信息，只能说：当伯克希尔现金占比异常高时，往往伴随宏观估值指标（Buffett Indicator、CAPE）也处于历史高位。后者作为长期回报预测因子，有较为稳定的实证支持——CAPE 在过去百年与未来 10 年实际回报有约 −0.5 到 −0.6 的横截面相关[9][10]。

If you must extract something usable from this phenomenon, the most you can honestly say is: Berkshire's cash share tends to coincide with elevated macro valuation indicators (Buffett Indicator, Shiller CAPE). The latter do have meaningful empirical support as long-term return predictors — CAPE shows a roughly −0.5 to −0.6 cross-sectional correlation with subsequent 10-year real returns[9][10].

但这是估值指标的预测能力，不是巴菲特现金堆的预测能力。前者样本充分（百年以上的横截面）、机制清晰（mean reversion of multiples）；后者只是它的二手代理，加上选择偏差和叙事光环的放大效应。

But that is the predictive power of valuation metrics, not the cash pile. The former has decades of cross-sectional data and a clear mechanism (mean reversion of multiples); the latter is just a secondhand proxy, amplified by selection bias and the narrative halo around Buffett.

实践含义 Practical Takeaway

想用数据预测美股走势：直接看 Shiller CAPE、长期实际利率、盈利增速。看伯克希尔现金堆 = 看一个比直接指标更嘈杂、还附带三个认知陷阱的二手信号。

Want data-driven views on US equity returns? Look directly at Shiller CAPE, long-run real rates, and earnings growth. Watching Berkshire's cash pile means watching a noisier proxy of those — with three cognitive traps thrown in for free.

方法论说明与局限 Methodological Notes & Limitations

样本量。n = 7 极小；任何"X 次中 Y 次"的命中率结果都不具备严格的统计显著性。在二项检验下，要拒绝"命中率 = 基准 13%"的零假设并达到 p < 0.05，至少需要约 30 个独立信号事件中 12 次以上"大跌"。本分析的目的是证伪而非证实——证伪一个流言只需要表明它没有比随机更好的预测能力，这一点 7 个样本已经足够说明。

Sample size. n = 7 is very small; no "X out of Y" hit-rate result reaches strict statistical significance. Under a binomial test, rejecting the null that hit rate equals the 13% baseline at p < 0.05 requires roughly 12+ "crashes" out of 30+ independent signals. The aim here is disconfirmation rather than confirmation — to debunk a claim, it suffices to show no better-than-random predictive power, and seven cases are enough for that.

独立性。2005 Q1 与 2005 Q4 高度相关（同一轮现金高位）。如果合并成单一事件，n 进一步降为 6，结论方向不变。

Independence. Q1 2005 and Q4 2005 are not independent (same cash-hoarding episode). Treating them as one event reduces n to 6; the conclusion direction is unchanged.

"现金峰值"的定义。这里用的是局部峰值，没有用一个固定阈值。如果改为"现金占比 ≥ 20%"等绝对阈值，2017 与 2019 两次会被剔除，但 2024 仍会触发，整体结论不变。

Definition of "peak." Local peaks rather than a fixed threshold. If we instead used an absolute cutoff like "cash share ≥ 20%," the 2017 and 2019 events would be excluded but 2024 would still trigger; the overall conclusion is unchanged.

价格回报 vs 总回报。本文使用价格回报。包含股息再投资会使各时期回报系统性提高约 1.5–2 个百分点，但事件 vs 基准的相对关系不变。

Price vs. total return. Price returns used throughout. Including dividend reinvestment shifts every horizon up by roughly 1.5–2 percentage points but does not change the event-vs-baseline comparison.
