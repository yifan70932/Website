Boeing 737 FMC / CDU

A simplified, browser-based interactive model of the Flight Management Computer Control Display Unit · 飞行管理计算机控制显示单元交互演示

Quick start

  1. Press RTE to open the route page.
  2. Type an origin airport (e.g. KBUF), then press the LSK next to ORIGIN (1L).
  3. Type a destination (e.g. KDFW), then press LSK 1R.
  4. The EXEC light turns green. Press it to activate the route.
  5. Press LEGS to view the generated lateral path. The active leg is shown in magenta; planned legs are white.

FlightAware
Live flight tracking + historical filed routes. Useful for seeing what routes airlines actually fly between two airports.

Detailed information on U.S. airports, navaids, and FBOs — runways, frequencies, fuel, communications.

Community database of flight plans across simulators (P3D, X-Plane, MSFS). Browse, generate, and export FMC-compatible routes.

Free virtual flight planning suite — generates OFP, fuel calculations, weather briefings, and FMC route strings used by sim pilots worldwide.

NOAA's official aviation weather portal: METAR, TAF, SIGMET, winds aloft, and graphical forecasts.

Digital Terminal Procedures Publication — official U.S. instrument approach plates, free and public-domain.

This is a teaching demo. The CDU paradigm and screen layout follow Boeing 737NG conventions, but logic, performance values, and the navigation database are heavily simplified. Distances and bearings are computed using the spherical-Earth great-circle formula (haversine). Cost index, climb/descent profiles, holding logic, and SID/STAR procedures are not implemented. Color coding follows real-aircraft conventions: magenta = active leg, cyan = pilot-entered or selectable, white = computed/inactive, green = activated/executed, amber = warning.
